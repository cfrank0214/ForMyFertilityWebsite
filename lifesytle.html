Stress

Studies suggests that women who are having difficulty conceiving take concrete steps to lower their stress levels :Pick up a cognitive behavior book which will teach you some stress management skills, see a therapist, or ask your primary care physician for a cognitive behavioral therapist. Get a relaxation CD and listen to it every day. There are a lot of things you can do on your own to reduce your stress.

Depression and/or anxiety

Depression and anxiety are known to hinder fertility. If you are suffering from either of these conditions, talk to your primary care physician to get a referral for treatment.

Smoking

Smoking affects every stage of the reproduction process – in both men and women. Men and women who smoke take longer to get pregnant than non-smokers. Second-hand smoke is almost as damaging as smoking and women who are exposed to second-hand smoke take longer to conceive than women who are not. The chemicals in cigarettes can cause damage to eggs and sperm which affects a future child's health. Women who smoke are:

  • more likely than non-smokers to have a miscarriage
  • babies have increased risk of low birth weight, being born
  • Passive smoking can affect the development of a baby girl’s ovaries.

Alcohol

Heavy drinking increases the time it takes to get pregnant and can affect a developing baby's health. For women planning a pregnancy, not drinking alcohol is the safest option.

Caffeine

Too much caffeine may affect fertility- by too much- think more than 2 8oz cups of caffeine a day- and don’t forget that caffeine is delivered in more than coffee and tea. Think beyond coffee to soft drinks and energy drinks.

Nutrition

both men and women can improve the chances of a pregnancy by eating a healthy diet. Best for the prospective parent, best way to give the baby a good head start. Think leafy vegetable high in folic acids and not French fries.
