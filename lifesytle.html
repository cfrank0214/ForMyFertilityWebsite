STI’s

Sexually transmitted infections are important preventable causes of infertility left untreated.

Stress

Studies suggests that women who are having difficulty conceiving take concrete steps to lower their stress levels

Depression and/or anxiety

Depression and anxiety are known to hinder fertility. If you are suffering from either of these conditions, talk to your primary care physician

Smoking

Smoking affects every stage of the reproduction process – in both men and women and smokers take longer to get pregnant than non- smokers.

Alcohol

Heavy drinking increases the time it takes to get pregnant and can affect a developing baby's health. For women planning a pregnancy

Exercise

A regime of good, active, aerobic exercise is shown to improve your fertility and reduce the stress associated with fertility worry.

Caffeine

Too much caffeine may affect fertility- by too much- think more than 2 8oz cups of caffeine a day.

Nutrition

Both men and women can improve the chances of a pregnancy by eating a healthy diet.
