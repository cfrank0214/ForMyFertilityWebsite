What affects sperm count?
  • Age:
    • The chance of having a child is much higher for women younger than 35 years and men younger than 40 years than for older women and men. Younger men have more active and better-quality sperm than older men. Most men make millions of new sperm every day, but men older than 40 have fewer healthy sperm than younger men. The amount of semen (the fluid that contains sperm) and sperm motility (ability to move towards an egg) decrease continually between the ages of 20 and 80.
  • Smoking
    • There is no safe limit for smoking, the only way to protect yourself and your unborn baby from harm is to quit. ***The chemicals in cigarettes damage eggs and sperm, and can affect a baby’s health.*** Men and women who smoke take longer to get pregnant than non-smokers. Second-hand smoke is almost as damaging as smoking and women who are exposed to second-hand smoke take longer to conceive than women who are not. The chemicals in cigarettes can cause damage to eggs and sperm which affects a future child's health. Smoking affects every stage of the reproduction process – in both men and women.
  • Weight:
    • Men who are overweight or obese have worse sperm quality and are not as fertile as men who are a healthy weight.
  • Sexual transmitted infections
    • Sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are common. People who have an STI often have no symptoms and can therefore have the infection for some time without being diagnosed or treated. STIs like chlamydia, gonorrhoea, syphilis, and mycoplasma genitalium can cause fertility problems. ***Some people with STIs don't experience any symptoms.**
  • Heat
    • Keep your testes cool if you want to be a father in the future. Testes need to be a few degrees cooler than the rest of the body to make good quality sperm. This is why they sit in the scrotum which acts like an ‘evaporative air conditioning system’, at a lower temperature than the rest of the body. If it gets too hot around the testes, they can have problems making sperm. Testes will start producing sperm again once the temperature of the scrotum stays at its normal lower level, which might take a few months.
  • Anabolic Steroids
    • Lifting weights and muscle building exercises increase muscle mass and strength and improve your bone health and your general health. Using anabolic steroids to increase muscle mass stops the production of sperm and may have other serious side effects.
Improve your sperm count
It takes about three months for men to make new sperm. So it’s really important to be as healthy as possible in the three months leading up to conception.
